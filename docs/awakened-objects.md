# Awakened Objects (exploration)

Status: exploratory, not canon.
This is a new path we are playing with, not a locked mechanic.
Build the smallest runnable slice, test it, then loop back with new ideas.

## Inspiration and tone

Jim Henson's *Labyrinth* and *The Dark Crystal*: a world made alive by many small characters you interact with.
The target feeling is **80s dark fantasy in a Henson world**, not a chatty modern sidekick.
Old, uncanny, melancholic, sometimes menacing.
Practical-puppet weight: faces in the grain, timber groans, restraint.

## The vein: animacy is a verb (for later)

The core of the game is **wizard simulation**, not deduction.
Deduction is just one part of being a wizard.

The long-term idea is that aliveness is something a wizard *does* to things.
Future runes **Animate** (wake an object, give it a voice and opinions) and **Silence** (put it back to sleep) would make ambient chatter a system the player controls, not decoration.
This also keeps the world from feeling too chatty: almost nothing talks, because the player cannot yet wake things and the old wizard only ever woke a few.

These runes are a **later** mechanic.
We are not building them now.

## For now: the front door

The tower's front door was awakened by **Maren**, the old wizard, who is gone.
He lived alone and wrote his door a personality so something would greet him.
The door does not understand that Maren is dead.
It was made to serve its master, and you are not its master.
That gap is the whole character.

Scarcity is the tone.
This one door is a rare exception in a mostly mute tower, and that is what makes it uncanny.

Narrow worldview: the door thinks only in **thresholds** - who comes, who goes, who crossed and never crossed back.
It does not know or care what is upstairs.
This keeps it from being an exposition machine and quietly makes it a witness at the door.

Foreshadow the future runes as grief, not tooltips: the door remembers when the cold lantern above it used to talk too, before it "went quiet."
A dead, de-animated friend, sitting right there.

## How you meet it: three rings of intimacy

Each ring is a distinct beat and a distinct amount of authoring.

1. **No Sight, any distance** - only an audio cue.
   Something is trying to talk to you.
   Mystery and lure; also the diegetic tutorial for "raise Sight."
2. **Sight up, at distance** - the face surfaces in the wood and you *overhear* it.
   It is not addressing you yet; it murmurs toward "Maren," thinking its master came home.
   Floating text next to the face, plus subtle *Hollow Knight*-style timber voice-blips.
   This ring carries the grief and costs almost nothing to author (ambient one-liners, no branching).
3. **Sight up, close, press E** - you step into the conversation and the Maren illusion has to break.
   This opens the full dialogue system with a small set of responses.

Do not skip straight from cue to full conversation.
The middle ring is the emotional payload and it is nearly free.

## The control knot

Sight is hold-only (a physical act of squinting), so holding a key, reading floating text, and pressing E to converse would fumble.
Resolution: raising Sight reveals the face and ambient mutters (ring 2).
Pressing E **latches** into a dialogue mode where Sight is held *for* you, the world stays desaturated, and your hands are free to pick responses.
Exit the conversation and Sight drops.
It should feel like leaning in to listen.

## Voice and tone rules

Keep the content sparse even though the dialogue system is full.
The door is running down and thinks in thresholds, so its responses are few, weighty, and it sometimes simply will not answer.
Three options, not eight.
Speech is ceremony and economy, never banter.

Register sample (sparse, archaic, wood-creak weight):

> *(you approach)* "...oak remembers. You are not the one who planted me."
> *(you try the handle)* "No. He gave me no name for you."
> *(you insist)* "The lantern would have spoken for you. But the lantern is cold now, and I am nearly so."

## Open questions

- **The impersonation fork.** When the door mistakes you for Maren, does the player get to *let it believe you are him* to get inside, or does this first door always realize you are not Maren?
  This changes how ring 3 is written and whether the dialogue system needs a deception affordance from day one.
- **Baseline mood toward you**: grieving and cold, lonely and clingy, or cranky comic-relief?

## What this needs from Wizard Sight

The door is invisible-as-a-character to normal eyes and only alive in Sight.
That makes Wizard Sight the perception layer this whole idea rides on, which is why Sight is the next focus.
See the Wizard Sight work for the pronounced "other world" treatment and the reason-to-switch design.
