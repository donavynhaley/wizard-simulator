## The Core Differences

**Focus & Tone**

Dark Fantasy (Modern): A broader umbrella that includes subgenres like "grimdark". It focuses on gritty realism, complex political struggles, 
anti-heroes, and  a generally hopeless outlook on the human condition. Modern examples include Game of Thrones or the Dark Souls 
video game franchise.

80s Dark Fantasy: Leans heavily into gothic imagery, witchcraft, the occult, and alien-esque monsters. It bridges the gap between children's 
fairy tales and genuine horror. Examples feature journeys into dreamlike, dangerous worlds without always needing a traditional "good vs. evil" 
plot line to follow.

**Visual Style**

Dark Fantasy (Modern): Heavily features CGI, visceral and realistic gore, and muted, desaturated, "realistic" color palettes to ground the 
fantasy in a gritty reality.

80s Dark Fantasy: Relies on elaborate, nightmare-inducing practical effects, puppetry, grainy VHS aesthetics, and neon or otherworldly 
color palettes.

**The "80's" Aesthetic Craze**

Because of its distinct look, 80s dark fantasy has surged in popularity online as a retro-futuristic or AI-generated aesthetic 
(often called simply "80s Dark Fantasy"). The modern fascination with it is a nostalgic nod to cult films of the era rather than a 
reflection of contemporary storytelling.

# Wikipedia's Takes on Dark Fantasy (*Not 80's Dark Fantasy In Specific*)

Dark fantasy or fantasy horror is a subgenre of literary, artistic, and cinematic fantasy works that incorporates disturbing and 
frightening themes. The term is ambiguously used to describe stories that combine horror elements with one or other of the standard 
formulas of fantasy.

A strict definition for dark fantasy is difficult to pin down. Gertrude Barrows Bennett has been called "the woman who invented dark fantasy".
Both Charles L. Grant and Karl Edward Wagner are credited with having coined the term "dark fantasy"—although both authors were describing 
different styles of fiction. Brian Stableford argues "dark fantasy" can be usefully defined as subgenre of stories that attempt to 
"incorporate elements of horror fiction" into the standard formulae of fantasy stories. Stableford also suggests that supernatural horror set 
primarily in the real world is a form of "contemporary fantasy", whereas supernatural horror set partly or wholly in "secondary worlds" should 
be described as "dark fantasy".

Additionally, other authors, critics, and publishers have adopted dark fantasy to describe various other works. However, these stories rarely 
share universal similarities beyond supernatural occurrences and a dark, often brooding, tone. As a result, dark fantasy cannot be solidly 
connected to a defining set of tropes. The term itself may refer collectively to tales that are either horror-based or fantasy-based.

**Anime and manga**

Berserk, a manga and anime franchise by Kentaro Miura that debuted in 1989, is frequently noted as an example of the genre due to its depictions 
of extreme violence, moral ambiguity, apocalyptic storylines and anti-hero protagonists.

Attack on Titan is a dark fantasy for its intense violence and the dystopian world it takes place in.

**Films**

Ridley Scott's film Legend (1985) has been described as a "dark fairy tale" fantasy film. Guillermo del Toro's fantasy film Pan's Labyrinth 
(2006) has been described as a "sort of a dark spin on Alice in Wonderland".

## General Overview

The major theme we are striving to achieve is an 80's dark fantasy. Exaggerated aspects of Horror, Fantasy, and Evil. The corrupting power
of dark magic is often a theme in this type of media and we should strive to show that where possible. Oppresive shadows, epic
quests, and , overwhelmingly etheral fantasy. Many of the images we want to conjure in the players mind can be found in those 80's 
dark fantasy tik-toks and films from the era and beyond listed below. Knights in plate armor; skeletal wizards in a creeky tower;
foggy days and twilight filled nights; the stars shimmering over a tall, cracked, stone spire; a smoke filled hall with a worried king, 
travelers by a campfire, long journeys along rocky hillsides. Fantastical spells that fill the screen with breathtaking visual effects. Goblins, 
orcs, fantastical beasts and and disturbing, alien like monsters. A surreal and almost dreamlike quality is seen in many of the 80's dark fantasy
media and style we are trying to replicate.

We want to achieve a period accurate but hyperbolic and exxagerated take on magic and occultism that delves into the research, hidden information, 
and esoteric connections only a wizard would be able to form with their unique knowledge and skills. Think rituals at night, gathering specific 
herbs for a potion, capturing lighting for a spell, etc. Environmental story telling will play a large part in not only the plot but the way you 
will learn different runes, spells, rituals, etc, throughout the game. Your tower itself will play a large part in the occult and the mysteries 
you seek to solve. As the tower will contain a large portion of the players game time we want to make it captivating simply to explore and live 
in. The magic has ties to historical thoughts on magic to focus our design and increase the immersion players feel. Exaggerated evil witches 
and mages of the time, bog hags, evil orcs in dark caves, etc.

The basis for many of the themes are medieval in nature, with the mythological thoughts of the time period heavily emphasized and embellished 
to create a more engaging story. Many fantasy stories of the time based the costumes, and set decorations on an image of 1500's Europe. Very
sword and sorcery vibes, any fantastatical elements we can think of can easily be added with only minor adjustments to make them darker and
draw out the surrealist nature of the fantasy if need be. Think mean, and corrupt dragons atop a hoard of stolen gold, a labyrinthian tunnel
system filled with minotaurs. Most of this will be world building but we do want to achieve that vibe.

Embrace the fantastical over the mundane where possible. Although the fantasy elements and dark, evil, villains might be over the top - the
characters and the setting take itself seriously. I want to try and focus on achieving that grainy, otherworldy, effect with the colors and
lighting of the game where possible. This is something we can easily change to heighten the mood as seen in many of the inspirations, where
when good is depicted it is a very, bright, grandiose, and hopeful moment shown in stark contrast of the darker scenes and imagery. 

We want the village to play a large part in the storytelling, think stardew valley or harvest moon. No unnamed characters, every npc has both
a purpose and a personality. The village should feel realistic but also exaggerated, guards are wearing plate armor, not simple cloth. The inn's 
are filled with dangerous rogues, and fancy bards, etc.

Many of the stories and films feature adventurers seeking out a mage or hermit like character for knowledge, we could try and incorporate that
into the questing, where our wizard isn't necessarily the main character in a great quest but the esoteric hermit sought out by the hero for help.
We could use this as a jumping off point for the corruption and morality system where you have the choice to either help or hinder the heroes as
you see fit to push your own goals and narratives.

# General Lore & Setting

The Kingdom of Cardea (kar-dee-ah) survives in the 398th Year of the Third era. It has been nearly four centuries since the end of Zilnar the 
Defiler's reign. He rose to power as an evil and corrupted lich who drove the world into anarchy for many years. His capture and subsequent
sealing away marks the beginning of the Third Era, a time of much rebuilding, yet the turbulent waters still wake. Troubles are often easier
found than one would like. The world itself seems to hide grotesque secrets in dark caves, and damp shadows. You must always be catious of that
which goes bump in the night. Mages are still sometimes looked at with suspiscion and a sense of weary-ness, however most wizards operate within 
the law and follow The Order of Mages. A connected series of schools intended to teach and prepare their students for a life of magic and 
servitude as a court wizard. They have a strict set of laws they must abide by in order to maintain their status as a lawful mage.

Followers of Zilnar still plot in secret for his return. One day through enough effort they may see their fallen leader rise once more and spread
his vile hatred upon the realm of Cardea. The Order of Mages have a secret society who hunts these vile worshippers. This society is known only
to those who call themsevles amongst their ranks. They are said to take upon great transformations that shift them into something more beast than 
man, yet they retain their faculties and use thier new senses to hunt and destroy Zilnar's followers. They are banished to a life of exile, even
most wizards are forbidden to learn of their exsistence.

The average day to day life of a citizen in Cardea is much less epic than one might imagine, they still have to do daily chores and maintain the
town, there is the occisional magical element even here though. Perhaps the mill in town is powered by a magical artifact and the wizard is the
only one who can work on or upgrade it. The blacksmith is run ragged trying to create arms and armor to bear with the nastiness of the world. 
A cryptic fortune teller sits under a water logged tent and spells out misery for all who are foolish enough to sit before her. The mead is made
with special berries that give you an extra little buzz.

As with any time in the medieval ages but especially in a time filled with dangerous magics and foul beasts, religion plays a large part in the
lives of all Cardean people, wizards not withstanding. The main gods worshipped by the people are simply called The Protectors, three mages who
sacrificed themselves to seal away Zilnar. They trained for so long, and grew so much knowledge their skills and presence on this plane was
indistinguishable from that of a God and so they are revered as such in The Third Era. Similar to the way Zilnar's followers revere their dead
patron as a God. 

*The people of Cardea can rest easy knowing our Gods won, right...?*

As a wizard it seen as your duty to study and reach the same level of power as The Protectors, so that should Cardea ever need those skills once 
more she shall be ready with an army to defend her from evil forces that may come. The Lords of Cardea know not much of this plan, but just
enough to sponsor and provide towers for wizards in their realms. It's always better to have a magic wielder nearby should something happen.

# Main Plot

After the local lords court wizard mysteriously vanished you have been assigned to take over the tower in the realm, after it was cleared of
all magical wrongdoing of course. As a new graduate of The Order of Mages, this is you first true task as a wizard. Should you do well, surely
you status in The Order will increase and one day you may even reach the coveted status of Arch-Mage. Until then you must work hard, see that
the village prospers, and you use your magical knowledge for good, and don't give in to the tempations of evil.

Should you do all this well, no problems shall arise for you, and you will grow old, wise, and very powerful. Should you fail in your task of
helping the village, or see yourslef driven mad with corruption, you have failed and must be executed as such. The Order of Mages holds no ill 
will toward you, as it does towards all the wizards and witches it teaches and polices, however they will not hesitate to see your driven to
the afterlife in pursuit of peace and preventing Zilnar's return.

As a new court wizard you must complete quests, maintain your dues, and complete independent studies for yourself.
